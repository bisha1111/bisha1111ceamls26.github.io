what_i_learned: |
  Today, our team focused on planning, scripting, and organizing the elevator pitch for the DermaBridge project. We discussed the main points to include, such as the problem, our proposed solution, and the purpose of the project.

  We also worked on arranging the script in a clear order. I learned that an elevator pitch should be short, direct, and easy for the audience to understand. We removed unnecessary details and focused only on the most important parts of the project.

  We also discussed how to divide the presentation among team members and make sure each section connects smoothly.

blockers: |
  One challenge was deciding which information was important enough to include in the short pitch. We had several ideas, but the presentation needed to remain clear and within the time limit.

  Another challenge was making the script sound natural instead of sounding like we were reading directly from a research paper. We also had to avoid repeating the same points.

reflection: |
  Today helped me understand the importance of planning before giving a presentation. A good elevator pitch needs to be organized, easy to follow, and focused on the main message.

  Writing and organizing the script also helped us explain DermaBridge in a simpler way. By the end of the session, we had a clearer structure and a better plan for presenting the project.
