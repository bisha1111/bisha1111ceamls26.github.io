what_i_learned: |
  Today focused on reviewing our model results, improving the research paper, exploring the DermaBridge app, and completing our Midsummer Symposium slides.

  We began by reviewing the results for EfficientNet-B0, ResNet50, and EfficientNetV2. We checked whether any final tuning was needed and made sure the benchmark table included the accuracy, AUC, and F1 score for each model. I learned that choosing the best model requires looking at several metrics, training behavior, and the types of errors shown in the confusion matrices.

  We then worked on completing the Methods section and reviewing the Introduction. The Methods section explains our datasets, preprocessing steps, models, training process, and evaluation metrics. The Introduction explains the problem DermaBridge addresses and why fairness, explainability, and trustworthy AI are important in skin-lesion screening.

  During the afternoon, our mentor showed us the current DermaBridge app scaffold. We reviewed the user interface, app structure, and how the trained model will connect to the application. I learned that the app must preprocess uploaded images in the same way as the training data before sending them to the model.

  We also discussed the goals for Week 6, including fairness auditing, bias mitigation, model refinement, and app development. We plan to compare true positive rate gaps across skin-tone groups and use tools such as Fairlearn and AIF360 to study possible bias.

  Later, we finalized our symposium slides and practiced our speaking sections. The slides had to be locked by the end of the day because tomorrow will be used only for the dry run. I practiced slowing down, improving my timing, and explaining technical ideas clearly.

blockers: |
  One challenge is making sure all three models were evaluated under the same conditions before comparing their results.

  Fairness evaluation is also difficult because skin-tone information is missing for many images in the combined dataset.

  Connecting the model to the app will require the preprocessing and input formats to match the training pipeline.

  I also need to continue improving my speaking pace and avoid relying too heavily on the slides.

reflection: |
  Today helped me understand how model evaluation, research writing, fairness testing, app development, and presentation preparation are connected.

  The app walkthrough showed me that a trained model is only one part of a complete system. The interface, preprocessing pipeline, patient information, and model output must all work together.

  Finalizing the slides also reminded me that a strong presentation should focus on the most important information and help the audience understand the project clearly.

  Overall, today was productive because we reviewed our models, improved the paper, explored the app structure, and prepared for the Midsummer Symposium.
