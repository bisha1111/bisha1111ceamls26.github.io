what_i_learned: |
  Today was our paper-writing day, with the morning focused on the abstract and introduction and the afternoon dedicated to the literature review and Python certification. We began by creating a placeholder for the abstract because it cannot be finalized until our model results are complete. This helped me understand that although the abstract appears first in a research paper, it is often one of the last sections to be completed because it must summarize the entire study accurately.

  We then started writing the introduction. Each student was assigned a specific paragraph, which allowed us to divide the work while still contributing to one complete section. I learned that the introduction should guide the reader from the general research problem to the specific purpose of the study. For our project, this includes explaining the importance of early skin cancer detection, the growing use of artificial intelligence in medical imaging, and the need for accurate and trustworthy skin lesion classification systems.

  In the afternoon, we began working on the literature review. I learned that a literature review should connect and compare previous studies instead of discussing every paper separately. The goal is to identify patterns in the research, including the models, datasets, methods, performance results, and limitations reported by different researchers. We also continued adding our sources and notes directly to the shared Google Drive spreadsheet so that everyone could follow the same format and easily locate the information later.

  Later, we worked on the HackerRank Python Basic certification course. The course reviewed core Python fundamentals and allowed us to practice solving programming problems independently. It required careful reading, logical thinking, and attention to the expected output. After working through the course, we shared our progress and certificates before completing our daily journal entries.

blockers: |
  One challenge was writing an assigned paragraph that could fit naturally into an introduction being completed by several students. I had to make sure my section stayed focused, avoided repeating ideas that another student might cover, and followed the same tone as the rest of the paper.

  Another challenge was deciding which details from the research papers were most important for the literature review. Some papers contained a large amount of information about their datasets, models, methods, and results, so I had to focus on the details that were most relevant to our project. The Python certification questions also required close attention because small mistakes in understanding the instructions could lead to an incorrect solution.

reflection: |
  Today reminded me that research is not only about preparing data and training models. Researchers must also be able to explain the problem, connect their work to previous studies, and communicate their findings clearly. Working on the introduction helped me think more carefully about why our skin lesion classification project matters and how to present that purpose to a reader who may not be familiar with the topic.

  Beginning the literature review also showed me the importance of comparing research rather than simply summarizing it. Looking at how previous studies used different deep learning models and datasets helped me better understand the progress already made in skin lesion classification. It also helped me recognize that a high accuracy score does not explain everything. The dataset, class distribution, evaluation method, and limitations of the study must also be considered.

  The Python certification course was a useful way to continue improving my technical skills while working on the written side of the project. Computer science research requires both programming and communication, so today gave me the opportunity to strengthen both areas. Overall, the session helped me become more confident in contributing to our research paper and organizing information from academic sources.
