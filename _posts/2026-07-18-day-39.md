what_i_learned: |
  Today, our team began developing the final presentation for the DermaBridge project. We discussed how to organize the slides so that the presentation clearly explains the problem we are addressing, our research process, the development of the application, and the progress we have made throughout the program.

  We also reviewed our main findings and started deciding which results, visuals, and project milestones would be most valuable to include. This process helped me understand that creating a strong research presentation requires more than simply placing information on slides. Each section must connect smoothly to the next, and the content must be understandable to both technical and nontechnical audiences.

blockers: |
  Our main challenge was deciding how to present the most important information without making the slides overcrowded or difficult to follow. Since the DermaBridge project includes machine learning, fairness, application development, and healthcare accessibility, we had to carefully select the details that best communicate the purpose and impact of our work.

  We also needed to determine how to divide the presentation among team members while maintaining a consistent structure and message.

reflection: |
  Beginning the final presentation gave us an opportunity to review everything we have accomplished and identify the strongest parts of the DermaBridge project. It also helped us recognize areas that may need additional explanation or supporting visuals before the presentation is complete.

  Moving forward, we will continue developing the slides, refining the content, and practicing how we will deliver each section. Our goal is to present DermaBridge confidently and clearly at the National Symposium on Effective and Ethical AI (N-SEA) while showing both the technical value and the potential social impact of the project.
