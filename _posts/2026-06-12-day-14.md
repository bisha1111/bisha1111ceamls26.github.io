what_i_learned: |
  Today was mainly focused on preparing for and completing our weekly showcase. The
  showcase gave us an opportunity to summarize everything our DermaBridge group had
  accomplished during the week, including the progress we made, the challenges we
  experienced, our overall project goals, and the tasks we plan to work on next.

  We had already started building the presentation yesterday, so during our group
  session today, we focused on completing the remaining slides, reviewing the
  information, and making sure everyone understood their speaking section. This
  preparation helped us organize the week into a clear story instead of simply
  listing every task we completed.

  We also reviewed some of the changes Blessing made to our code. Going through the
  updates helped me understand how the code had been improved and why certain
  adjustments were necessary. It also reminded me that reviewing changes made by
  another team member is an important part of collaboration because everyone needs
  to understand the current version of the project.

  During the showcase, I learned more about the work being completed by the other
  research groups. Each group was working on a different problem, so listening to
  their presentations exposed me to new applications of artificial intelligence
  and different approaches to research.

  We also discovered that another group was using the ResNet50 model, which is one
  of the models our team has been working with. Since we had already gained some
  experience using it, we were able to share a few observations about the model and
  explain some of the challenges we had encountered. It felt good to contribute
  something useful to another group based on what we had already learned.

blockers: |
  I did not experience any major technical blockers today because most of our work
  involved reviewing the code and preparing the presentation. Our group completed
  the slides on time, and the showcase went smoothly without any serious problems.

  My main challenge was feeling nervous while presenting. Because I wanted to make
  sure I covered everything within the time available, I began speaking too
  quickly. This caused me to stumble over some of my words even though I understood
  the information I was presenting.

  I know this is something I can improve with more practice. Before the next
  showcase, I plan to rehearse my section several times, slow down while speaking,
  and pause between important points. I also want to become more comfortable
  presenting without feeling as though I need to rush through every sentence.

reflection: |
  Today was a good and productive day. Our presentation went smoothly, and the
  group was able to explain our weekly progress without any major hiccups. I was
  proud of how we worked together to finish the presentation and make sure each
  person was prepared before the showcase began.

  Although I was nervous and spoke faster than I intended, I am not discouraged by
  it. Presenting is a skill that improves with experience, and each showcase gives
  me another opportunity to become more confident. I am glad that I still completed
  my section and continued speaking even when I stumbled over a few words.

  I especially enjoyed listening to the other groups because it allowed me to see
  how much everyone had accomplished during the week. Hearing about their projects
  also showed me that many groups may use similar tools or models even when they
  are working on completely different problems. The conversation about ResNet50
  made the showcase feel more collaborative because we were able to share what we
  had learned instead of only presenting our own work.

  We ended the day earlier than usual so that everyone could begin preparing for
  the transition to campus and the start of the in-person portion of the program.
  I am excited about finally working with everyone face-to-face and experiencing a
  different routine from our virtual sessions.

  Dr. Mack also gave us a preview of what we will be doing on Monday, including a
  data analytics workshop. That sounds interesting because it connects closely to
  the dataset exploration and machine-learning work we have already started with
  DermaBridge.

  Overall, today gave me time to reflect on how much progress we made during the
  week. I am looking forward to beginning the in-person portion of the program,
  meeting everyone on campus, and continuing to improve my research, coding, and
  presentation skills.
