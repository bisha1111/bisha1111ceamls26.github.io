what_i_learned: |
  Today was our first day meeting in person for the summer research program. We
  began by reviewing important program dates, expectations, upcoming activities,
  and the schedule for the remaining weeks. This helped me better understand what
  we will be working toward and how each workshop connects to our research
  experience.

  During our first workshop, Dr. Dacon introduced us to data analytics. We worked
  with a breast cancer dataset, created arrays, examined the information, and
  generated graphs to represent the data visually. The workshop helped me connect
  some of the Python and data-preparation skills I have been practicing with the
  work we are doing for DermaBridge. I especially enjoyed seeing how raw medical
  data can be organized and transformed into visual information that is easier to
  understand.

  We also participated in a workshop about errors and bias in artificial
  intelligence models. We examined examples from popular AI systems and discussed
  whether the problems came from structural bias, limitations in the training
  data, or coding errors that could have been addressed during development. This
  workshop showed me how easily an AI model can produce unfair or inaccurate
  results when certain groups are not properly represented in the data.

  This topic was especially relevant to DermaBridge because our project focuses on
  building a trustworthy skin-lesion screening system that considers fairness
  across different skin tones. I learned that developers must evaluate not only
  the overall performance of a model but also how well it performs for different
  groups of people.

blockers: |
  One challenge today was keeping up with all the steps during the data analytics
  workshop. Some parts moved quickly, so I had to pay close attention while
  creating the arrays and graphs. I plan to review the code and practice working
  with similar datasets so that I can become more confident with the process.

  Another challenge was helping students who were not familiar with Morgan State
  University locate the BSSC building. Although this created some confusion at the
  beginning of the day, it also gave us an opportunity to support one another and
  work together.

reflection: |
  Today was an enjoyable and meaningful start to the in-person part of the program.
  It was exciting to finally meet the other students, my research group members,
  and our mentors face-to-face after previously communicating online. Being
  together in person made the program feel more real and helped me feel more
  connected to the team.

  I also enjoyed the marshmallow challenge and the other group activities because
  they encouraged communication, creativity, and teamwork. Spending time with my
  group members allowed us to become more comfortable with one another and begin
  building stronger working relationships.

  The workshops helped me see how data analytics, responsible AI, and teamwork all
  connect to the research process. I left the day feeling motivated and excited
  about what we will accomplish with DermaBridge by the end of the summer.
