what_i_learned: |
  Today felt like the real beginning of our research work. We learned how to search
  for academic articles online, identify reliable sources, and extract useful
  information that could support our research topic. I practiced looking beyond
  the title of an article and paying closer attention to its purpose, methods,
  findings, and connection to the problem we are studying.

  This activity helped me understand that research requires more than simply
  collecting information. It involves reading carefully, comparing different
  sources, taking organized notes, and deciding which ideas are relevant to the
  project. I also learned that using credible sources is important because the
  information included in our work must be accurate and supported by evidence.

  We also practiced solving Python problems. These exercises helped me strengthen
  my understanding of Python syntax and improve the way I approach coding
  challenges. I learned that breaking a problem into smaller steps makes it easier
  to understand what the code should accomplish before I begin writing it.

  In addition, I learned more about Figma and how it can be used to plan the design
  of an application before development begins. We explored how to organize content,
  arrange interface elements, and create layouts that are clear and easy for users
  to understand. This showed me how design decisions can affect the overall user
  experience of an application.

blockers: |
  My main challenge today was getting comfortable with Figma. Since I am still new
  to the platform, it took me some time to understand how to arrange elements,
  organize different sections, and turn an idea into a clear visual layout. I need
  more practice using the design tools and making sure that each part of the
  interface has a specific purpose.

  I also found it challenging to decide which information from the articles was
  most important. Some articles contained a large amount of technical information,
  which made it difficult to separate the main findings from the smaller details.
  I plan to improve by focusing on each article's purpose, research methods, major
  findings, limitations, and connection to the DermaBridge project.

reflection: |
  Today showed me that research involves much more than coding. It also requires
  finding reliable information, understanding previous studies, organizing ideas,
  and thinking carefully about how a final product will look and function for its
  users. Each of these areas contributes to the development of a strong research
  project.

  I enjoyed seeing how research, Python, and Figma can work together. The research
  articles provide the knowledge and evidence behind the project, Python supports
  the technical development, and Figma helps us visualize how users may interact
  with the final application.

  This experience made me more excited about the DermaBridge project because I
  could begin to see how our early research and design work may eventually become
  part of a useful application. I am looking forward to improving these skills,
  becoming more confident with the tools, and contributing more as we continue
  developing the project.
