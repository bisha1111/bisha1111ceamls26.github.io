  Today, my team participated in the Mid-Summer Research Presentation and presented the progress we have made on the DermaBridge project. We discussed the purpose of our research, the tasks we have completed, the difficulties we have worked through, and some of our early results.

  Presenting our work allowed us to demonstrate how much the project has developed since the beginning of the internship. It was also a valuable opportunity to explain our research to faculty members, mentors, and other student researchers.

Some teams explored air-quality monitoring, LEGO assembly, disaster prediction, healthcare, and other creative solutions to real-world problems. Seeing these different applications helped me better understand the wide range of possibilities within AI research.
