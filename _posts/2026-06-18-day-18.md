what_i_learned: |
  Today was our Showcase Day, so the schedule combined technical learning, presentation preparation, and the SAIRI Weekly Showcase. We began the morning by working through the Python course on Kaggle Learn. The lessons were short and practical, which allowed me to review important Python concepts at my own pace while completing as much of the course as possible.

  Working through the course helped reinforce concepts I have already encountered during the DermaBridge project. Instead of only reading explanations, I had to apply what I learned through small coding exercises. This gave me more practice with Python syntax, variables, functions, conditionals, lists, and other skills that are useful when preparing data and working with machine-learning code. I also saved my progress so that I could continue working toward the completion certificate.

  Later in the morning, we attended a virtual Summer Lecture Series session led by Dr. Shaun Gittens. The lecture gave us an opportunity to learn outside our immediate project responsibilities and hear another perspective on research and professional development. It reminded me that becoming a stronger researcher involves more than completing technical tasks. It also requires listening to experienced professionals, asking questions, and remaining open to new ideas.

  During the afternoon, our attention shifted completely to preparing for the DermaBridge presentation. We reviewed our slides, updated the sections that needed improvement, and made sure the presentation accurately represented the progress of our application and research. We also checked that the slides followed the same visual structure and that each section connected naturally to the next.

  Before the showcase, the entire team completed a full dry run. We practiced our speaking order, timing, transitions, and responses to possible questions. This was especially helpful because it showed us where explanations needed to be clearer and where we were spending too much time. At the end of the day, we presented DermaBridge during the SAIRI Weekly Showcase to the full institute.

blockers: |
  One challenge was balancing the Python coursework with the pressure of preparing for the showcase. I wanted to understand the lessons instead of rushing through them, but I also needed to remain available for slide revisions, team discussions, and presentation practice.

  Another challenge was deciding how much technical information to include in the presentation. Our project involves datasets, image preprocessing, machine-learning models, patient metadata, fairness, and application development. We could not explain every detail within the available presentation time, so we had to focus on the most important information and communicate it in a way that the audience could understand.

  Timing was also an important concern during the dry run. Each team member needed enough time to explain their section without causing the presentation to run over the limit. We had to improve our transitions, remove repeated information, and make sure everyone understood when to begin speaking.

  Presenting to the full institute also made me nervous because I wanted to represent our team's work accurately. However, practicing beforehand helped me feel more prepared and allowed me to focus on delivering my section clearly.

reflection: |
  Today showed me how closely technical skills and communication skills are connected. The Python course strengthened the foundation I need to understand and contribute to our code, while the showcase required me to explain our work clearly to people who may not have the same technical background.

  I learned that a successful research presentation is not simply a collection of completed slides. Every team member must understand the purpose of the project, know how their section connects to the overall story, and be prepared to answer questions. Our dry run helped us identify weaknesses before the final presentation and reminded me that preparation can make a major difference in confidence and delivery.

  Presenting DermaBridge to the full institute was an important moment because it allowed us to share the progress we have made so far. Our project is still developing, but the showcase gave us an opportunity to explain the problem we are addressing, the work we have completed, and the direction we plan to take next.

  I was especially proud of how our team worked together throughout the day. Everyone contributed to polishing the slides, improving transitions, and preparing for questions. Although the schedule was busy, the experience helped me become more comfortable speaking about research and presenting technical work in a clear and organized way.

  Overall, Day 18 strengthened my Python knowledge, presentation skills, confidence, and ability to work under pressure. It also reminded me that research is not complete until the work can be communicated effectively to others.
