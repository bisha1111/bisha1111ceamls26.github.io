what_i_learned: |
  Today was our final virtual session with Dr. Pandey, and it was a really meaningful way to close out the series. Her sessions helped me understand that research writing is not just about putting information together, but about being precise, organized, and intentional with every word. One of the biggest things I took away was the difference between a conference abstract and a paper abstract. I learned that even small details, like tense and wording, matter depending on whether the research is still in progress or the results have already been completed.

Another important lesson was how to identify a research gap and build an introduction that clearly leads the reader to the purpose of the study. I also gained a better understanding of how AI tools can support academic writing, but only when they are used responsibly. We discussed the importance of documenting prompts, tracking revisions, and reviewing AI-generated responses carefully instead of accepting them without question. That reminded me that strong research requires patience, honesty, and attention to detail.

In my project lab, I presented on Decision Tree Regressor, which helped me explain how machine learning models use patterns in data to make predictions. We also discussed Random Forest Regressor and how using multiple decision trees can make predictions more reliable and stable. Connecting these models to our own dataset made the concepts feel more practical. It also showed me how much clean data and proper preprocessing can affect the performance of a machine learning model.
