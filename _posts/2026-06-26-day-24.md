what_i_learned: |
  Today was focused on preparing for the upcoming Mid-Summer Research Presentation. The nine research teams were divided into three smaller groups, giving each team an opportunity to practice presenting before the official event on Monday.

  My team presented the current progress of the DermaBridge project. We discussed our research goals, accomplishments, challenges, and preliminary results. This practice allowed us to test how clearly we could explain our project and determine whether the different sections of our presentation connected smoothly.

  After presenting, we received helpful feedback from Elijah, one of the program coordinators. He pointed out areas where we could improve the organization, clarity, and overall flow of the presentation. His suggestions helped us better understand how to make our research easier for an audience to follow.

  We also watched the other teams present their projects. It was interesting to learn about their research topics and observe the different ways they organized and delivered their presentations. I gained a few ideas that could help my team improve our own slides and speaking approach.

blockers: |
  I did not experience any major technical problems today. Our main challenge was deciding how to organize the information so that our research story remained clear, focused, and easy to understand within the presentation time.

reflection: |
  Today's practice session showed me how important constructive feedback is when preparing a research presentation. Although our team had already completed a strong draft, Elijah's comments helped us recognize small improvements that could make our presentation more effective.

  Watching the other teams also helped me think more carefully about presentation style, slide organization, and audience engagement. The session made me feel more confident and prepared for the official Mid-Summer Research Presentation.

  Over the weekend, our team will use the feedback we received to revise our slides, improve our transitions, and continue practicing. I am looking forward to presenting the progress we have made on the DermaBridge project on Monday.
